    <h3>About this course</h3>
    <p> Welcome to Computer Science and the endless opportunities it offers!  </p>

    <p>Computer science opens more doors than any other discipline in today’s world. Learning even the basics will help you in virtually any career—from architecture to zoology.  Just as you may have learned how to dissect a frog, or how to write an essay, it’s important for every 21st century student to learn how to write a program and to understand their role in a world that increasingly relies on computers. </p> 
    
    <p>In AP Computer Science, you will no longer be a passive consumer of technology—you will become a developer who actively harnesses current technology to develop new technologies. You will learn how to create useful programs and apps that can be applied to solve problems; you will learn how to create games that can be shared with your friends.</p>

    <p>AP Computer Science Principles offers a multidisciplinary approach to 
learning the underlying principles of computation. In this course you will be introduced to the creative aspects of programming, abstractions, algorithms, large data sets, the Internet, cybersecurity concerns, 
and computing impacts. AP Computer Science Principles will also provide you 
the opportunity to use current technologies to create computational artifacts for both self-expression and problem solving. Together, these aspects of the course make up a rigorous and rich curriculum that aims to encourage participation in computer science.</p>

    <h3>Prerequisites</h3>
    <p>AP Computer Science Principles is for anybody interested in expressing themselves creatively. Whether or not your are pursuing a career in technology, computer science is a “must have” skill in our economy. </p>
    
    <p>If you have strong math skills or programming experience, you should consider AP Computer Science A. If you are just curious and may not have as strong of a background in computer science, AP Computer Science Principles is the course for you!</p>

    <h3>Credits Earned</h3>
    <p>AP Computer Science Principles may count as an elective credit. It may also be substituted for a math credit.</p>
